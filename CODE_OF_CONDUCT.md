# Code of Conduct

This document defines the standards of behavior expected from everyone who participates in the OhMyOpenSource! community - contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and anyone interacting in our repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, or any other space managed by the organization.

---

## Our Commitment

We are committed to maintaining a community that is welcoming, respectful, and productive for everyone, regardless of age, background, nationality, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, religion, or level of technical experience.

Contributing to open source should be a positive experience. This code of conduct exists to make that the default, not the exception.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- **Be respectful**: disagree with ideas, not people. Critique code, not contributors.
- **Be constructive**: feedback should help the recipient improve, not make them feel unwelcome.
- **Be patient**: not everyone has the same context, experience, or availability. First-time contributors deserve the same respect as long-time maintainers.
- **Be collaborative**: open source works because people share. Share knowledge, share context, share credit.
- **Assume good intent**: most misunderstandings come from miscommunication, not malice. Ask before assuming.
- **Stay on topic**: keep discussions relevant to the work at hand. This is a technical community, not a general-purpose social space.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following will not be tolerated in any space managed by OhMyOpenSource!:

- Harassment, intimidation, or threats of any kind, public or private
- Discriminatory language or behavior based on any personal characteristic
- Personal attacks or insults, including dismissive remarks about someone's level of experience
- Deliberately hostile or inflammatory language in code reviews, issue threads, or comments
- Publishing private information about another person without their consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions or collaborative processes
- Any other behavior that a reasonable person would consider unprofessional or unwelcoming

---

## Code Review and Disagreement

Technical disagreements are normal and expected. How they are handled is what defines the culture of a project.

When you disagree with an approach:

- Explain your reasoning clearly and back it up with evidence or experience
- Acknowledge the tradeoffs in your own position
- Accept that the other person may be correct

When your work is criticized:

- Separate the feedback from your identity - it is about the code, not about you
- Ask for clarification before responding defensively
- Recognize that a reviewer pointing out a problem is doing you a service

No one should feel attacked for making a mistake or for having a different opinion. Disagreements should result in better decisions, not in one party feeling unwelcome.

---

## Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this code of conduct, please report it by opening a private communication with the organization maintainers.

All reports will be handled with discretion. The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed without explicit consent.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct. In response to a confirmed violation, maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate, including:

- A private or public reminder of expected behavior
- A warning with conditions for continued participation
- Temporary or permanent removal from the community spaces managed by OhMyOpenSource!

Enforcement decisions are made in good faith, with the goal of protecting the community, not punishing individuals.

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all spaces managed by OhMyOpenSource!, including GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, code reviews, and any other official communication channel. It applies to all participants, regardless of their role.
